Chad le Clos Announces Comeback Bid for 2028 Olympics

June 15, 2025 By Sports Correspondent

South African swimming legend Chad le Clos has announced his intention to compete at the 2028 Los Angeles Olympics, potentially making him a five-time Olympian at age 36.

The Comeback Plan

Le Clos revealed his ambitious training regimen and modified technique that he believes will keep him competitive against swimmers nearly half his age. "I still have the fire," le Clos told reporters in Cape Town.

A Storied Career

The 2012 Olympic champion in the 200m butterfly famously defeated Michael Phelps in London. With 18 World Championship medals and multiple Commonwealth Games records, le Clos remains Africa's most decorated swimmer.

Key Highlights

  • 2012 Olympic Gold Medalist (200m butterfly)
  • 4-time Olympic medalist
  • 18 World Championship medals
  • Commonwealth Games record holder
  • First African swimmer to medal in three consecutive Olympics

Olympic Changes and New Opportunities

The 2028 Games will feature new events like the 50m butterfly that may favor le Clos's sprinting ability. Changes in swimming suit technology could also benefit veteran swimmers.

Inspiring the Next Generation

"I want to show African kids that age is just a number," le Clos said. "Our swimming programs are producing world-class talent, and I hope my journey continues to open doors."
