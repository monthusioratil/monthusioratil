Botswana's Zebras Secure AFCON 2025 Qualification

June 12, 2025 By Tshepo Moloi

In a historic night at the National Stadium, Botswana's national football team, the Zebras, secured their place in the 2025 Africa Cup of Nations with a dramatic 2-1 victory over Egypt.

Decisive Match Against Egypt

The Zebras came from behind to defeat the Pharaohs in front of a sold-out crowd in Gaborone. After going down 1-0 in the first half, second-half goals from Tumisang Orebonye and Thatayaone Ditlhokwe sealed the victory. The winning goal came in the 87th minute, sending the nation into raptures.

Group Stage Opponents

Botswana has been drawn in Group D alongside Nigeria, Algeria, and Uganda. While it's a challenging group, coach Didier Gomes da Rosa believes his team can surprise. "We're not going just to participate," he said. "We've shown we can compete with the best in Africa."

Key Highlights

  • First AFCON qualification since 2012
  • First ever competitive victory over Egypt
  • Finished top of qualifying group with 13 points
  • Only conceded 3 goals in 6 qualifying matches
  • Striker Orebonye finished as group's top scorer with 5 goals

National Celebration

Thousands of fans took to the streets across Botswana after the match, with the largest celebrations in Gaborone and Francistown. President Mokgweetsi Masisi declared a public holiday, saying "This is a moment of immense national pride that unites all Batswana."

Looking Ahead

The Botswana Football Association has announced a series of high-profile friendlies to prepare for the tournament in Morocco. There are also plans to upgrade facilities at the National Stadium to meet CAF standards. With the team's current form and the nation behind them, expectations are high for a strong showing at AFCON 2025.
